Post-Workout Recovery

What to eat within an hour after your workout if not having a recovery snack or shake
Recover with plenty of fluids along with a snack that contains at least 20 grams of protein* (preferably dairy for a good source of leucine) and double the amount of carbohydrates (40-50 grams**). This supports muscle protein synthesis, optimal energy replenishment and re-hydration.

*20 grams of protein = Two 8 oz glasses of white milk or chocolate milk
**A 3.5-ounce plain bagel contains about 50 grams of carbohydrates

Examples

Greek Yogurt, Granola, & Dried Fruit Peanut Butter & Jelly Sandwich Assorted Nuts & Apple Sauce Assorted Nuts & Dried Fruit Turkey Sandwich and Pretzels 16 oz Chocolate Milk & 1 Banana Waffle with 2 Eggs or Peanut Butter Trail Mix with Chocolate, Cereal, & Dried Fruit Protein Shake* Protein Bar*

Protein Bars & Shakes

Tips
Post-Workout Bars: Must have 20 grams of protein (whey/casein), and 30-50 grams of carbohydrates.

Don't worry about sugar, your body will use it

Include 8 ounces of low-fat milk for added protein and carbohydrate for a complete post-workout recovery

Post-Workout Shakes: Must have double the amount of carbohydrate. This can be done by adding fruit and yogurt.
