Workout Hydration

Consider factors including your sweat rate, the heat and humidity in your environment, and how long and hard you are exercising.

Before Workout
Drink 17 to 20 ounces of water 2 to 3 hours before you start exercising
During Warmup
Drink 8 ounces of water 20 to 30 minutes before you start exercising or during your warm-up
After Workout
Drink until you are no longer thirsty, as soon as possible (no more than 30 minutes after you exercise)

At least 8-10 ounces of water
Goals & Measurement
The goal of hydration is to maintain weight or regain any weight lost during your workout.

If you're participating in a sport such as football, soccer, lacrosse, or hockey where you might be weighing before and after workouts to assess fluid lost, remember it's 3 cups of water for every pound lost.
